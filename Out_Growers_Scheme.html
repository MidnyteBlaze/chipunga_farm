Growing Together, Prospering Together

Chipunga Farm's Outgrowers Scheme is a partnership program that empowers local farmers to become successful commercial growers of premium coffee, macadamia nuts, and honey.

Through this initiative, we provide smallholder farmers with the resources, knowledge, and market access they need to transform their farming operations into profitable enterprises. Together, we're building a sustainable agricultural community that benefits everyone.

How Our Outgrowers Scheme Works

A step-by-step partnership model designed for your success

01

Registration & Assessment

Interested farmers register with us. Our team visits your farm to assess suitability and discuss your goals.

02

Training & Capacity Building

Comprehensive training on best practices for coffee, macadamia, and beekeeping from our agricultural experts.

03

Inputs & Support

Access to high-quality seedlings, beehives, equipment, and ongoing technical support throughout the growing cycle.

04

Monitoring & Guidance

Regular farm visits and guidance to ensure optimal growth, pest management, and quality production.

05

Harvesting

We provide guidance on optimal harvest times and techniques to ensure premium quality produce.

06

Guaranteed Market

We buy back all quality produce at competitive, transparent prices with prompt payment.

Specialized Programs by Product

Tailored support for each of our premium products

Coffee Program

Our flagship program focuses on premium Arabica coffee production. We provide disease-resistant seedlings, shade management training, and wet mill processing facilities.

  • High-yielding, disease-resistant seedlings
  • Training on organic farming practices
  • Access to centralized pulping stations
  • Premium prices for specialty grade

Macadamia Program

Our macadamia program supports farmers from nursery to harvest, focusing on high-quality nut production for export markets.

  • Pest and disease management support
  • Access to international markets

Beekeeping Program

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about joining our scheme

Any smallholder farmer with land suitable for coffee, macadamia, or beekeeping can apply. We prioritize farmers committed to quality and sustainable practices.

Registration is free. Inputs like seedlings and beehives are provided on a cost-share basis with flexible payment terms after harvest.

We offer competitive, transparent pricing based on quality grades and market rates. Prices are communicated clearly before each season.

We provide training, technical advice, quality inputs, and regular farm visits from our extension officers throughout the growing cycle.
